EXHIBIT 02

How the Experiment Works

Five steps stand between a bowl of soap water and a bubble within a bubble.

  1. Prepare the soap solution

    Dish soap, water, and a touch of glycerine — glycerine slows evaporation so the film survives longer.

  2. Create the first bubble

    A wand or ring is dipped and drawn through the air, stretching a thin liquid film into a sphere.

  3. Insert a soap-coated straw

    The straw is wetted with soap solution first, so it can pierce the outer film without popping it.

  4. Blow carefully

    A slow, steady breath through the straw pushes air into the space inside the first bubble.

  5. A second bubble forms inside

    A smaller bubble inflates within the first — two independent films, sharing one wall of soap.
